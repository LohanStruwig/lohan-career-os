# CNC Intel Hub

## Executive Snapshot

CNC Intel Hub is a vertical CNC/VMC market-intelligence product developed through VentureLayer Labs to support structured company review, territory context, dealer/customer fit, evidence-backed research, saved views, and exportable planning outputs.

The work demonstrates founder-led product capability, AI-assisted development, technical-commercial execution, data governance, workflow design, and practical delivery discipline.

## Context

B2B industrial markets often depend on fragmented company research, territory knowledge, channel context, sales judgment, and planning materials that can be difficult to structure consistently.

CNC Intel Hub addresses that environment by organizing market-intelligence work into a more repeatable product workflow.

## Challenge

The challenge was to convert complex CNC/VMC market context into a usable digital workspace that could support focused review, filtering, evidence, saved views, and exportable outputs without losing the reasoning behind decisions.

## Role

Lohan directed the product through VentureLayer Labs as founder/product owner, shaping product scope, workflow definition, feature prioritization, QA review, data governance, deployment discipline, and client-readiness decisions.

## Approach

The product approach centered on a practical market-intelligence workflow:

- define the company review process
- connect territory context to product behavior
- structure evidence-backed research
- support search and filter behavior
- preserve saved views
- prepare exportable planning outputs
- use AI-assisted planning and implementation support with human approval gates
- keep QA and data governance visible throughout the workflow

Technical fluency applied across React, TypeScript, Vite, Supabase, Vercel, GitHub, pdf-lib, Python, PowerShell, PostgreSQL, and SQL governance.

## Capability Demonstrated

This case study demonstrates product ownership, B2B market-intelligence systems, AI-enabled workflow design, technical-commercial execution, data governance, QA discipline, and GitHub-based delivery cadence.

During an intensive build cycle, the project recorded approximately 500 commits in one month, reflecting sustained product-development momentum and iterative execution.
